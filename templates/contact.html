Frequently Asked Questions

When placing an order, vendors can propose a price. Farmers can accept, reject, or counter-offer. Both parties can negotiate up to three times. If an agreement is reached, the order is confirmed. If no agreement is reached after three rounds of negotiation, the order is automatically rejected.

Farmers need to upload a valid farming license or agricultural registration document. The verification process typically takes 1-2 business days. Once verified, farmers can start listing their produce and receiving orders.

Once an order is accepted, the platform calculates the total amount, deducts a 5% commission, and processes the payment. The remaining 95% is transferred to the farmer. Payment details are provided after order acceptance. We support various payment methods including bank transfers and digital wallets.

The minimum order quantity is 200kg for any product. This requirement ensures that we focus on bulk B2B transactions. If you need smaller quantities, we recommend contacting local retailers.

Daily Mandi shows historical price trends for various products. It aggregates data from all transactions on our platform and displays changes over time through interactive charts. This helps both farmers and vendors make informed decisions about pricing and purchasing.
